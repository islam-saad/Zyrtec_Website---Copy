• Managing Allergies

Allergy occurs when your immune system overreacts to something that is harmless to most people but triggers a reaction in anyone sensitive to it. This substance is known as an allergen.1

Allergic reactions vary from person to person. They can range from minor irritation to a life-threatening emergency called anaphylaxis.2

The Quality Of Life

Some allergies may interfere with your day-to-day activities or impact your quality of life.3

  • Symptoms can occur in spring, summer, and early fall.
  • They are usually caused by sensitivity to airborne mold spores or to pollens from trees, grasses, or weeds.4
  • Symptoms occur year-round and are generally caused by sensitivity to dust mites, pet hair or dander, cockroaches, or mold.4

Effective Management Of Allergy

  • Avoiding things that you're allergic to is your best option to manage allergies.5
  • But some common allergens can be difficult to be avoided. Your healthcare provider might recommend that you take certain medications daily or get specific treatments regularly.5
  • Depending on your allergies, options could include:5
Antihistamines
Steroid nasal spray
Leukotriene modifiers
Injections for food allergies or hives
Allergy immunotherapy
